Working of muscles while doing Horse Stance

Estimated Time- 30 seconds

The horse stance is a common posture in Asian martial arts and takes its name from the position assumed when riding a horse. It is called mǎbù in Chinese, kiba-dachi in Japanese, and juchum seogi or annun seogi in Korean. This stance can not only be integrated into fighting but also during exercises and forms. It is most commonly used for practicing punches or to strengthen the legs and back.
